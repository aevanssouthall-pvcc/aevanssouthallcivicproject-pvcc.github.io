Overview

It is inarguable that the advancement of computer technology has changed society indefinitely. This technology opened up so many job markets in every single career field, while also paving the way for many other technological advancements. As great as these things are, many argue that computer technology has its limitations, which has prevented us from making even more monumental changes in the world. Is this true? To what extent would we be better or worse off without the creation of computer technology?

Topic is derived from Question #3 from Chapter 2 of Brookshear Textbook

Let's lay out some of the pros and cons of computer technology.

PROS:

Here is a list of several pros that have come from the development of computer technology:

  • The Internet
  • Job Opportunities
  • Productivity

Now let's break each of these topics down

The Internet:

The introduction of the internet is one of the most monumental developments caused by computers. The ability to connect to anyone, anywhere has changed the way people view the world. Additionally, the Internet allows for vast amounts of information to be easily accessible to ordinary people. Whether it be scholarly articles, wikipedia, or youtube, people can find information on seemingly endless topics using the internet

Job Opportunities:

The development of computer technology has also expanded the job market exponentially. Every career field can benefit from the use of computers, whether it be retail cashiers, engineers, or marketers, the implications of computers are endless. An article written by the National Bureau of Economic Research states that, “The rapid spread of computer technology in the workplace may explain as much as 30 to 50 percent of the extra growth in the demand for more-skilled workers since 1970”

Productivity:

Computers have also streamlined productivity in many fields. A great example of this is a calculator, which is able to immediately solve math problems, massively saving time. Computers are also great for making databases or analysing large amounts of data quickly, allowing companies to automate otherwise tedious processes, massively increasing productivity.

CONS:

While the implications of computer technology have many positives, it is also very important to mention and understand some of the negative effects the technology can have. Here is a list of some of those negative effects:

  • Cybercrime
  • Technological Obsolescence

Cybercrime

With the rise of computer technology, there has also been a rise in malicious activity. With computers often being used to store and transmit sensitive information, hackers and other malicious people seek to prey on computer users for their own personal gain. For example, phishing attempts are becoming more and more commonplace, targeting gullible people to click on links that download viruses or potentially scamming them. With the rise of computers, its important to know the risk you’re at while using the technology.

Technological Obsolescence

As society becomes more and more reliant on computers in everyday life, technological obsolescence as a risk becomes more and more real. For the past few decades, we have been building our infrastructure on technology that quickly becomes outdated and a potential security risk. For example, in the medical field, it is very important to avoid technological obsolescence. As medical technologies quickly advance, patient data is put at risk.

Conclusion:

Let's look back to the original question of “to what extent would we be better or worse off without the creation of computer technology?” While yes, there would be no more worry over security risks or the implications of technological obsolescence, we would also lose ease of access to near infinite amounts of information, as well as the interconnectivity provided by the Internet. We would lose all of the increases in productivity computers are able to provide, while also scaling down the jobs that computers gave us access to. I believe that, ultimately, we would be worse off without the creation of computers.
